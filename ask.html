Recent Answers

How do I stop spiralling when anxiety hits out of nowhere?

Star: The key is understanding that your brain is doing what it was trained to do. You don't fight the spiral — you step outside it. Notice the thought, label it ("my brain is doing the anxiety pattern"), and immediately take a valued action. Even something small like making your bed or texting someone you love. You're teaching your brain that YOU choose what's important, not the anxiety. This is the foundation of mental fitness.

I've been in therapy for 2 years but still feel stuck. Is your approach different?

Star: Therapy is valuable but it often focuses on understanding WHY you feel a certain way. Mental fitness focuses on WHAT you do about it. I teach practical tools you can use in the moment — cognitive defusion, valued actions, the value garden practice. Most of my clients and readers say they got more from these simple tools than years of traditional approaches. It's not about replacing therapy — it's about adding the fitness layer.

How do I build self-worth when I don't feel like I deserve it?

Star: Here's the thing most people get wrong — self-worth isn't a feeling. It's a practice. You build it by doing valued actions REGARDLESS of how you feel. Show up for yourself even when your brain says you're not worth it. That's the workout. Every time you do something aligned with your values despite not feeling like it, you're literally rewiring your brain's self-worth pathways. Start small. Be consistent. The feeling follows the action, not the other way around.
